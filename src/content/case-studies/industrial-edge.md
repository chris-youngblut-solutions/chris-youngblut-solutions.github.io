Two related field artifacts in agricultural/industrial edge work — a diagnostic and a curriculum — plus the public protocol library they connect to.

## raven-viper — a GPS-dropout diagnostic

A diagnostic for intermittent GPS dropout on a self-propelled agricultural sprayer's GPS-guided steering system. The problem is the kind that resists a clean fix: the dropout is intermittent, and the symptom (the steering loses its position fix) sits at the end of a signal path with several possible upstream causes.

The diagnostic isolates the dropout along that path — receiver, bus, steering controller — to separate a hardware fault from a firmware or configuration cause. Each segment is checked in turn so a single intermittent symptom resolves to a located cause rather than a guess, and the same fault, when it recurs, points to the same segment.

Field equipment and the equipment-specific particulars are kept general here. What the artifact demonstrates is the method: take an intermittent failure on a real bus-connected machine, decompose the signal path, and attribute the fault to a segment.

## sprayer-apprenticeship — a field curriculum

A documentation-only curriculum that walks a small group through the sprayer firmware-flash procedure end to end. It is the second half of the same job: once a fix exists, it is written down so others can perform it without the person who first worked it out.

The curriculum covers each step of the flash procedure, what the step does, what can go wrong at that step, and how to verify the step succeeded before moving on. The structure turns a one-time fix into a teachable, repeatable procedure — failure modes named in advance, and a verification check at each stage so a mistake surfaces where it happened rather than at the end.

It is documentation only — a written procedure, not a tool or a deployed system. Equipment brand and firmware-version specifics are kept general.

## The public anchor: opendbc-ag

The domain's public proof is **opendbc-ag** — an MIT-licensed agricultural CAN-bus protocol library. The diagnostic and curriculum are the field side of the work; opendbc-ag is the protocol side, and it is the part that is public and inspectable.

### What it is

opendbc-ag is a community-maintainable repository of CAN DBC files for agricultural equipment — the ag analog to comma.ai's automotive `opendbc`. Its scope is pure-standard PGNs only: ISO 11783 (ISOBUS) public summaries and the SAE J1939 ag-relevant subset. No reverse-engineered proprietary OEM PGNs. A DBC file maps raw CAN frames to named signals, so the corpus is the thing that makes a captured ag-equipment bus readable.

It fills a gap. There is no widely shared MIT-licensed ag-CAN DBC corpus comparable to comma.ai's `opendbc`. The commercial incumbent is closed and non-redistributable; community projects each maintain partial DBCs without a shared baseline. opendbc-ag aims to be that baseline — content anyone can fork, modify, and ship.

### Coverage

The corpus totals **2,690 unique PGNs and 2,780 signals** across three DBC files, each from a distinct public source:

- **iso11783_from_agisostack.dbc** — 46 frames, 84 signals, derived from AgIsoStack++ (MIT). Signal-rich: speed/distance, guidance, time/date, language, maintain-power, heartbeat.
- **iso11783_from_vdma.dbc** — 2,627 frames and signals, from the isobus.net VDMA Data Dictionary. PGN-level only; the signals are `_RawPayload` placeholders awaiting community signal-level enrichment.
- **j1939_ag_subset.dbc** — 17 frames, 69 signals, a hand-curated SAE J1939 ag-relevant subset; signal-rich.

The signal count is honest about its shape: 2,627 of the 2,780 signals are PGN-level `_RawPayload` placeholders from the VDMA file, awaiting community PRs. The developed, signal-level definitions live in the AgIsoStack and J1939 DBCs. A live coverage matrix is auto-regenerated by CI on every DBC change.

### How the corpus is built

Each DBC file is produced by a dedicated extractor that writes an intermediate JSON first, then converts that JSON to DBC via `canmatrix`. The two-stage pipeline keeps generation reproducible and the intermediate auditable: an AgIsoStack++ C++ extractor, a VDMA HTML scraper, and a hand-curated J1939 list each feed their own JSON, which becomes the matching DBC. Every signal in the final artifact carries a comment naming its public source, so provenance traces from the shipped DBC back to the original published page or line of code.

### Scope enforced by CI, not by review

Three CI workflows run on push and pull request:

- **ci.yml** — a Python 3.10/3.11/3.12/3.13 matrix; installs the package, parses every DBC via canmatrix, and runs the full test suite (76 passing, 2 xfailed for documented inferrer edge cases). A separate soft-fail `audit` job runs pip-audit. Hard fail blocks merge.
- **consistency.yml** — rejects any PGN ID in the proprietary `0xEF00` or `0xFF00..0xFFFF` ranges, and any PGN ID appearing in more than one DBC file. Hard fail blocks merge.
- **coverage.yml** — on push to `main`, regenerates the coverage doc and commits the diff. Best-effort, informational.

Two principles drive that design. Scope policy is enforced by code, not by review: anyone can open a PR, and CI rejects scope violations without a maintainer reading every line. And duplicate detection is enforced by code: cross-DBC duplicate PGN IDs are easy to introduce by accident when three extractors target overlapping ranges, so the build makes it an error rather than a runtime correctness bug.

The test suite breaks down across nine files — smoke/parse invariants, the centralized scope predicate (28 tests), signal-naming rules, the heuristic inferrer, the coverage generator, the log anonymizer, and the three extractor parsers. A synthetic CAN fixture is regenerated by a deterministic script (byte-identical output) and seeds known signal patterns so the inferrer's heuristics can be checked against ground truth.

### Scope policy and the legal frame

The scope decision is deliberate and documented. In scope: PGNs in standard ISO 11783 / J1939 ranges, sourced from public summaries. Out of scope and CI-enforced: PGNs in the proprietary `0xEF00` and `0xFF00..0xFFFF` ranges. Out of scope by policy: transcribed paywalled-spec text, reverse-engineered OEM proprietary frame definitions, and material whose primary purpose is circumvention.

The project ships a legal-context document that situates this against the ag repair-rights landscape — the FTC's 2025 action against Deere, the federal FARM Act, Iowa HF 2763, and the DMCA §1201 exemption process (the tractor-software repair exemption granted in 2021 and renewed in 2024). The document is explicit that it is context, not legal advice. The public-standards-only scope is chosen partly because it is unambiguous across every plausible regulatory outcome: a focused scope is simpler to maintain, lowers contributor coordination overhead, supports downstream use as a dependency, and stays stable as the repair-rights debate resolves.
